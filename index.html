SKS Builder 팀 미팅 — 웹사이트 유지관리, SOW 협상, Configurator 브리핑 2026.04.16
🔤 주요 어휘
💬 회의 표현
📌 비즈니스 용어 (Terms)
SOW Term
업무 범위 계약서 (Statement of Work)
"We have four SOWs and this one has ended."
Retainer Term
장기 유지관리 계약 (월별/연별로 지속 계약)
"the website retainer"
Scope Term
업무 범위
"adding her on the BPO scope"
Amendment Term
계약 수정안, 부속 계약
"they did sign the 2025 amendment"
Out-of-pocket cost Term
직접 발생 비용, 실비
"our only responsibility was the out-of-pocket cost at about $60K"
RFP Term
제안 요청서 (Request for Proposal)
"not like a traditional RFP where you're bidding it out to three people"
GP (Global Procurement) Term
글로벌 구매팀 — 계약·가격 승인 권한 보유
"GP has to do their process"
Brief Term
프로젝트 지시서 (업무 요청 문서)
"I have to put it in words into the brief"
Proof of performance Term
성과 증빙 자료 (예산 사용 결산용)
"it's called a proof of performance"
Fund reserve Term
예비 예산 (계획된 지출을 위해 확보해둔 금액)
"in my budget log, I have the fund reserve"
Internal transfer Term
내부 이동 (타부서에서 온 직원)
"she's an internal transfer, which is great"
Traffic (team) Term
에이전시 내 리소스·일정 관리팀
"I asked traffic — I'll get that for you"
Activity review Term
활동 결산 보고 (예산 집행 내역 정리)
"I have to do an activity review to close it out"
Configurator Term
웹 기반 제품 커스터마이저 툴
"Configurator 2.0"
Parallel path Term
동시 병행 진행
"we can parallel path that"
Fire drill Term
갑작스러운 긴급 업무
"we had some other fire drills with our department head"
🔁 구동사 (Phrasal Verbs)
close out Phrasal
마무리하다, 종결하다
"I have to do an activity review to be able to close it out."
💡 "finish"보다 공식적 종결 뉘앙스
figure out Phrasal
파악하다, 알아내다
"I'm trying to figure out when I need to jump in versus not."
💡 "understand"보다 더 적극적인 탐색 뉘앙스
jump in Phrasal
바로 끼어들다, 개입하다
"I didn't really jump in as much because GP handles it."
step in Phrasal
개입하다, 나서다 (공식적·의도적 개입)
"I need to step in and figure out what's going on."
💡 jump in보다 더 무게감 있는 개입
bump up Phrasal
올리다, 높이다
"Maybe I should have bumped that up knowing they'd ask for a discount."
layer in Phrasal
추가로 얹다, 단계적으로 덧붙이다
"We can layer in her packages."
💡 기존 위에 쌓아올리는 느낌
swap out Phrasal
교체하다, 바꾸다
"Swapping out the product, you need the packages."
💡 "replace"보다 캐주얼하고 실무에서 자주 씀
hand over / hand off Phrasal
넘겨주다, 인계하다
"I'm going to hand that over to Amy."
fill in Phrasal
① 빠진 부분을 채우다 ② 누군가를 대신하다
"Can you fill in for SKS?" / "fill in the brief"
reach out Phrasal
먼저 연락하다
"I'm going to reach out to Samuel."
💡 비즈니스 영어 필수 표현
carve out Phrasal
바쁜 중에 시간을 내다
"It's just carving out the time to do it."
throw (it) into Phrasal
(AI·툴 등에) 넣다, 돌리다
"I throw it into my AI of choice."
pull Phrasal
(정보·데이터를) 가져오다
"Pull these two emails and make the brief."
come up on Phrasal
(마감이) 다가오다
"We are coming up on the deadline with no signed scope."
put my head down Phrasal
집중해서 묵묵히 일하다
"I'll just put my head down and work through what I can."
🧠 고급 표현 (Advanced Expressions)
manage expectations Expression
기대치를 조율하다, 관리하다
"I feel like we need to manage expectations around the timing."
💡 상대가 너무 많이 기대하지 않도록 미리 조율
squared away Expression
깔끔하게 정리된, 해결된
"Until we have that sort of squared away..."
back and forth Expression
주고받기, 왔다갔다하는 소통
"The back and forth Samuel and I were having."
to a T Expression
정확히, 딱 맞게
"The cost is to a T of what the work will be."
💡 "exactly" 대신 쓰면 훨씬 자연스러움
morph (significantly) Expression
상당히 변화하다, 다른 형태로 바뀌다
"It has morphed quite significantly over the years."
grow exponentially Expression
기하급수적으로 커지다
"It has grown exponentially — from $60K to $320K."
be aligned with Expression
~와 방향이 맞다, 동의하다
"Make sure that they're aligned with the work."
navigate (a situation) Expression
복잡한 상황을 헤쳐나가다
"How are we going to navigate this conversation?"
💡 "deal with"보다 훨씬 세련된 표현
provide background Expression
배경 설명을 하다, 컨텍스트를 깔아주다
"I could provide some background of how we got here."
a whole plethora of Expression
엄청나게 많은, 다양한
"They give you a whole plethora of packages."
follow (her) lead Expression
~의 리드를 따르다
"I'm following her lead on the Matterport."
surmise Expression
추측하다 (근거 있는 추측, 고급 어휘)
"I'm surmising this based on the line of questioning."
💡 "guess"보다 훨씬 격식 있고 분석적인 뉘앙스
⚡ 본론 전에 먼저 짚고 싶을 때
"I just wanted to give that callout before we jump in."
본론으로 들어가기 전에 먼저 말씀드릴 게 있어요.
I just wanted to give that callout before we jump in — there's a timeline issue we need to be aware of.
⏸️ 보류·일시 정지를 제안할 때
"We're going to put a pause on that."
일단 그건 보류하려고 해요. (완전 취소는 아닌 뉘앙스)
The budget's not confirmed yet, so let's put a pause on the vendor conversation for now.
🤔 확실하지 않은 의견을 낼 때
"I'm surmising this based on the line of questioning."
질문들의 흐름을 보고 추측하건대...
I'm surmising this based on what I've seen, but I think they're looking for a price reduction.
ℹ️ 상대가 몰랐을 정보를 알려줄 때
"Just for your edification..."
참고로 말씀드리면... / 혹시 모르실까봐...
Just for your edification, the scope has actually grown from $60K to $320K since 2023.
📍 중요 포인트를 강조하고 싶을 때
"I just wanted to flag that." / "I wanted to call that out."
이 부분을 짚고 싶었어요.
I just wanted to flag that we still haven't received the signed SOW.
🔀 두 작업을 동시에 진행하자고 할 때
"We can parallel path that."
그건 동시에 병행해서 진행할 수 있어요.
You work on the builder brief and we can parallel path the SKS portion with Amy.
🕐 당장 답 못 하고 유보할 때
"I'll have that conversation and get back to you."
그 얘기 나눠보고 다시 연락드릴게요.
I'm not sure what Samuel's thinking yet — let me have that conversation and get back to you.
🛎️ 선제적으로 정보를 공유할 때
"Just in case it comes up, I want you to be in the know."
혹시 이슈가 생길 경우를 대비해서 미리 알려드려요.
Helen asked about Configurator 2.0 — just in case it comes up, I want you to be in the know.
🎯 특정 관점에서 말할 때
"From a business perspective..." / "From a pricing standpoint..."
비즈니스 관점에서 보면... / 가격 측면에서는...
From a business perspective, we can't wait any longer for the decision.
✅ 적극적인 의지를 표현할 때
"I'll make it a priority."
우선순위로 처리할게요.
I know you need the brief by Friday — I'll make it a priority this week.
📊 현재 상황을 정리할 때
"That's where we're at."
현재 상황이 그렇습니다.
We've submitted everything but haven't heard back from GP — that's where we're at.

📅 일별 수집 표현 계속 업데이트 중
2026.04.17
"We can connect on Monday."
월요일에 얘기하자 / 월요일에 미팅 잡자
"connect"를 동사로 사용. "meet"이나 "talk"보다 캐주얼하고 자연스럽게 미팅을 제안하는 표현. 이메일 마무리나 회의 말미에 딱 어울림.
"I'll pull together the numbers — we can connect on Monday to go over it."
"It wasn't heart to heart."
진심이 통하지 않았다 / 진솔한 대화가 아니었다
"heart to heart"는 진솔한 대화를 뜻하는 표현. 부정형으로 쓰면 소통의 진정성이 없었다는 뉘앙스.
"The meeting went fine on the surface, but it didn't feel very heart to heart."
